  • You should try to undertrack a strafing opponent. This means trying to track the trailing edge (closest to the center of their strafe) of the opponent and letting them strafe back into your bullets. If you can do this is reduces the reaction time component, as you have the entire time it takes them to move across your crosshair before you have to react to a change of direction. Easier said than done, of course. Both clips you were beaten by AD spam. AD spam has a fixed, mostly symmetrical strafe. If you recognize someone is AD spamming you can cheat more at aiming. You know roughly the area they are going to stick to, so you don't have to track outside this. You can even hold your crosshair mostly steady and just shoot them as they cross it. The smoother you are the easier it is to see what you are aiming at, and the easier it is to track. You were jerking the crosshair around a lot here. One other trick is to look at how you initiate the fight. It's much easier to track them if you start on target. In the first clip they were already AD spamming by the time you were on target. In the second your tracking was slightly off at the start. With a bit more accuracy you could have done 100 to that Ash before they had time to react.
